1. Introduction to Cameras:
    . A camera works on the principle of passive light sensors to produce a digital image of a covered region.
    . Cameras are capable of detecting both moving and static objects within their surrounding environment.
    . Cameras can see colors, textures, and can identify road signs, traffic lights, lane markings...
    . High processing power is required to analyze camera images data.

2. Types of Cameras for ADAS:
    . Monocular camera: contains only one vision capturing array and processing module.
    . Stereo camera: contains two or more lenses with a separate image sensor for each lens.

3. Applications of Cameras in ADAS:
    . Traffic sign recognition system
    . Speed limit recognition system
    . Blind spot detection
    . Lane departure warning and prevention system
    . Lane keep assistance
    . ...
